# Narration Rules

How to write the `narration` field on each slide. Used (Phase 3+) to synthesize
voiceover and drive karaoke highlighting. (Sourcing: `plan/03`, `research/04`.)

## The fundamental rule
**Complement, don't repeat.** Never read the slide text verbatim. The slide shows
the takeaway; the voice adds the *why, how, and story*.

## Pacing
- ~130–150 words per minute.
- **~20–40 words per slide** for a tight explainer (tune per slide; cover/CTA can
  be shorter).
- Short sentences. One breath each.

## Per-slide micro-structure
`hook → point → bridge to next`. Example:
> "Here's the catch. (hook) Most teams lose people at step three of onboarding.
> (point) So we rebuilt that step first. (bridge)"

## Voice & style
- Conversational, second person, contractions ("you'll", "it's").
- Signpost: "First… / Here's the key part… / Which brings us to…".
- Speakable: expand symbols and acronyms on first use; avoid unpronounceable
  tokens, long URLs, or code in narration.

## Highlight-aware writing (important)
The karaoke effect highlights on-slide words as they're spoken. So **make sure the
key nouns/verbs you want to light up actually appear in both the slide text and
the narration**. If the slide title says "40% drop-off", say "forty percent
drop-off" (or include "drop-off") in the narration so the matcher can sync it.

## Open & close
- **Cover:** one strong hook line; set the tone.
- **CTA:** a clear, single action ("Try it", "Star the repo", "Book a demo").

## Length guard
Keep the whole narration tight. A 10-slide deck at ~30 words/slide ≈ ~2 minutes of
video — ideal for an explainer.
